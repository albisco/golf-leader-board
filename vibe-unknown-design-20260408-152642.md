## Problem Statement

Golf day organizers — particularly those running events for multiple organizations
(schools, corporates, community groups) — manage entire golf days with paper scorecards
collected at the end and tallied by hand. There is no live leaderboard during play,
no interaction between groups on the course, and results take an hour to produce after
play ends. The organizer's reputation is on the line with every new client.

## Demand Evidence

Firsthand observation of the current workflow:
- One master spreadsheet or paper scorecards per group
- Manual tallying at end of day — slow, error-prone, disputed results
- No live leaderboard during play — players have no idea where they stand
- No interaction between groups spread across 18 holes
- Results arrive so late that momentum and excitement from the round is already gone

The organizer runs this for multiple clients per year. A botched event damages a
client relationship, not just an internal gold star.

## Status Quo

Pure analog. Paper scorecards collected at the end of the day, tallied by hand.
Slow, error-prone, results come an hour after play ends. No real-time information
available to any player during the round.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary user:** A community/corporate golf day organizer who runs events for multiple
organizations — schools, corporates, community groups. Their reputation is their
business. Purchase decision happens before the event. Value proven during the event
(client sees live leaderboard, group banter on their phone). Renewal locked in after
("can we do that again next year?").

**Narrowest wedge:** One organizer, one real event, end-to-end. They create the event,
groups scan QR codes, scores sync to a live leaderboard with inter-group chat. The
client sees it and says "wow." That's the whole experiment.

**Player persona:** Often casual golfers at a corporate or community day. Won't
download a native app. Must join via browser link or QR code with zero friction.

## Constraints

- Zero install for players — browser/QR only
- Sub-10-minute event setup for the organizer
- Must work reliably during a live event (WebSocket connections stable across 4 hours)
- In-app group chat with notifications — players don't have each other's contact
  details, so external channels (WhatsApp) don't work

## Premises

1. Primary user is a multi-event organizer (not a single golf club). Pain = event
   setup speed + impressing each new client.
2. Browser/QR-first, zero install — with in-app group chat + browser notifications
   as the re-engagement mechanism. Players won't check back voluntarily; the product
   must pull them back to see leaderboard drama.
3. Competitive advantage is setup-to-live-leaderboard speed — under 10 minutes
   from scratch to live event.
4. The product creates EVENT ATMOSPHERE, not just scoring. Inter-group banter, live
   leaderboard drama mid-round, memorable moments. This is what makes the organizer
   look like a hero to their client.

## Cross-Model Perspective

Independent Claude subagent cold read:

"This is an event production tool disguised as a golf app. The real product is
'make any organizer look like a pro in front of any new client, every time.'
The moat is not scoring accuracy (solved problem) but the social/atmosphere layer

Key insight: "Their reputation is their business" is the entire product strategy.
Every feature should be evaluated through the lens: does this make the organizer

Challenged premise 2 (browser-only): the real risk is not install friction but
engagement drop-off mid-round. A browser link has no pull mechanism. Resolved by
building in-app notifications and group chat into the platform itself.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Minimum Viable Event (scoring + live leaderboard only)
- Effort: S | Risk: Low
- Pros: Shippable in 2-3 days, validates core scoring workflow
- Cons: Misses the stated differentiator; looks like every other scoring app
- Completeness: 5/10

### Approach B: Full Event Hub — scoring + live leaderboard + group chat (chosen)
- Effort: M | Risk: Med
- Pros: Delivers the differentiator, creates shareable moments, defensible vs
  existing apps
- Cons: 1-2 weeks to build; moderation risk (banter can go wrong); notification
  permission UX needs care; iOS Safari push notification limitations require fallback
- Completeness: 7/10 (open questions around auth and moderation remain)

### Approach C: Organizer Platform First (multi-event dashboard)
- Effort: L | Risk: High
- Pros: Addresses multi-client need, creates switching costs, clear B2B SaaS path
- Cons: Doesn't solve player experience first; long time to first real event
- Completeness: 7/10 platform / 3/10 player experience

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B: Full Event Hub**

Start with the player experience — QR join, score entry, live leaderboard, group
chat. The organizer's client sees it working during a real round. The social layer
(banter, notifications, leaderboard drama) is the differentiator. Build that first.
The multi-event organizer dashboard comes in v2 once the player experience is proven.

### Real-time flow
```
Player submits score via HTMX form
  → FastAPI writes to Neon (Postgres)
  → WebSocket broadcast to all connected clients
  → HTMX swaps leaderboard HTML fragment in-place
  → Chat message appears without page reload
```

### Format: 4-Ball Ambrose

Each group is a team of 4 players. On each hole, all 4 players hit from the tee,
the best shot is selected, and all play from that spot — repeated until holed. The
group records one gross score per hole (the team score).

**Net scoring:**
- Net total = sum of gross scores across all 18 holes − group handicap
- Group handicap is an integer set manually by the organizer per group (not
  calculated from individual player handicaps)
- Lower net total wins
- Individual player handicaps are recorded for organizer reference only (v1)

**Mid-round leaderboard display:**
- Groups are at different holes simultaneously; direct net comparison mid-round is
  misleading. Show: gross total so far + holes completed (e.g. "68 gross — 12 holes")
- Final ranking (all 18 holes complete): net = gross total − group handicap, sorted
  ascending. Incomplete groups ranked below complete groups.

### Data model (core tables)
```
events        id, name, date,
              format ENUM('ambrose_4ball'),   -- v1 only; enum supports future formats
              organizer_id, join_code,
              hole_count INTEGER DEFAULT 18,  -- configurable: 9, 16, 18, etc.
              status ENUM('draft','active','closed'),
              created_at

holes         id, event_id, hole_number (1–N where N = events.hole_count), par (3|4|5),
              notes TEXT NULL    -- reserved for future special hole rules; NULL in v1
              -- stroke_index removed: not needed for net total scoring

groups        id, event_id, name (auto: "Group 1"…"Group N"),
              group_handicap (integer, set by organizer),
              qr_token

players       id, group_id, name, handicap (integer), is_scorer BOOLEAN
              -- individual handicaps informational only in v1
              -- one player per group flagged as scorer

scores        id, group_id, hole_id, gross_score,
              created_at, updated_at, edit_count
              -- ONE score per GROUP per HOLE (not per player)
              -- edit_count + updated_at = audit trail for corrections

chat_messages id, event_id, sender_name, content, created_at
              -- event-wide; all groups see all banter
```

### Player identity (no account required)
On first QR scan, player sees a one-field prompt: "What's your name?" Stored in
localStorage keyed to the group token. Used as `sender_name` in chat. No password,
no email. If localStorage is cleared, prompt appears again — acceptable for a
same-day event.

### Score entry: one scorer per group
The organizer assigns the scorer per group during setup (checkbox next to a player
name in the group editor; defaults to the first player listed). `is_scorer=true`
is set at that point — no runtime election by the group. The QR code scanned by the
scorer shows the full scoring interface; all other group members who scan the same
QR see a read-only view of their group's current scores.

Score entry form shows all group members. Scorer taps gross score per player per
hole and submits one hole at a time. Edit button on submitted holes lets the scorer
correct a score; logs `updated_at` and increments `edit_count` for organizer
audit trail.

**Handicap precision:** v1 stores handicap as integer. Decimal handicaps (e.g. 14.5)
are truncated to 14 at input. Acceptable for casual corporate events; exact WHS
calculations are a v2 concern.

### DNF / incomplete scorecards
Groups that do not complete all 18 holes are ranked below all complete scorecards
on the leaderboard, sorted by holes completed descending. Their row shows:
"Group 3 — 12 holes (incomplete)". Included in final results with the note;
no scores discarded. Net score calculation only applied to groups with all 18
holes entered.

### Fast course setup
Stroke_index is no longer required (net total scoring, not per-hole). This removes
18 fields from setup. Organizer enters:
- Event name, date (2 fields)
- Number of groups (1 field; auto-names Group 1–N)
- Par per hole: 18 fields, pre-filled to par 4; organizer adjusts only the par-3s
  and par-5s (typically 4–8 edits for a standard course)
- Group handicap per group (1 field per group)
- Players + individual handicaps per group (4 names + 4 handicaps per group)
- Assign scorer per group (1 checkbox per group)

For a 5-group event (20 players): ~45 fields total. Achievable under 10 minutes.
10-minute target is stated as: "under 10 minutes for a 5-group, 18-hole event."

### Organizer auth (magic-link)
Organizer enters email → receives a magic link → clicking it sets a session cookie
(7-day expiry). The magic link email stays in their inbox as a backup re-auth
mechanism. If session expires mid-event (phone battery/device switch), organizer
re-opens the email and clicks the link — no password needed. The link can be
re-used within its expiry window (24 hours). On device switch, the same link
re-establishes the session. This covers the "battery dies during a live event"
scenario.

### Event lifecycle
```
draft   → organizer sets up event, adds players, generates QR codes
active  → organizer taps "Start Event"; scores accepted; leaderboard live
closed  → organizer taps "Close Event"; scores locked; final results displayed
```
Score submissions rejected if `event.status != 'active'`. Leaderboard remains
readable after close — public URL stays live as the permanent results page.

### WebSocket reconnection
- Client reconnects on disconnect with exponential backoff: 1s, 2s, 4s, 8s, max 30s
- Server sends a heartbeat ping every 30s; client responds with pong
- On reconnect, client requests a full leaderboard snapshot (not delta) to catch
  up any missed updates
- v1 is single-instance only (no Redis pub/sub). Horizontal scaling requires Redis
  fan-out — deferred to v2.

### Core screens
1. **Organizer: Create Event** — name, date, format (4-ball Ambrose), number of
   groups (auto-named Group 1–N), par per hole (18 fields, default par 4), group
   handicap per group, players + individual handicaps per group, assign scorer per
   group (checkbox), generate one QR code per group, tap "Start Event" to go live
2. **Group Leader: Score Entry** — open QR link → enter name (first load only) →
   see 18-hole scorecard with one gross score field per hole → tap in score after
   completing each hole → submit; edit button on submitted holes for corrections
   (logged with updated_at + edit_count)
3. **Live Leaderboard** — single public URL shareable to all players and the client;
   WebSocket auto-refresh; during round: rows show "gross so far — N holes" sorted
   by holes completed then gross ascending; final (all 18 complete): net = gross −
   group_handicap, sorted ascending (lowest net wins); CSS row reorder transition
   200ms ease; incomplete groups shown below with "(N holes)" tag; event chat feed
   below the table
4. **Event Chat** — text input on leaderboard page; event-wide banter visible to all;
   sender_name shown; browser notification prompt on first interaction; iOS Safari
   fallback: in-app unread badge (no push, tab must be open)

## Open Questions

- **Tiebreaker:** Resolved — lower gross total wins. If gross is also tied, shared
  placing. No countback logic required.
- **Moderation:** v1 ships with no word filter. Organizer has a "delete message"
  button visible only to them. Word filter is a v2 decision.
- **Organizer auth:** Magic-link email for v1. Full account system deferred until
  multi-event dashboard is needed.
- **Multi-event dashboard trigger:** Build when the first organizer asks "where do
  I see my past events?" — not before.
- **Other formats:** Stableford, Medal deferred to v2. 4-ball Ambrose is v1 only.

## Success Criteria

- An organizer creates a live event in under 10 minutes using a standard par 72 preset (measure setup time)
- Players open the leaderboard link more than once during the round (target: >40%
  of players check back mid-round — this validates the atmosphere layer)
- The organizer's client says "can we do this again next year?"
- Zero score disputes at the end of the day

## Future Considerations (explicitly deferred)

These are real ideas worth capturing. Do not build them until v1 has been run at
a real event and the core loop is validated.

**Special hole rules (e.g., "Alphabet hole")**
A designated hole where a non-standard rule applies — e.g., players must hit in
alphabetical or reverse-alphabetical order by first name. The `holes.notes` field
reserves a slot for this in the data model. The UI to communicate and enforce the
rule (showing the required shot order, confirming compliance) is a v2 feature.
Enforceability via software is limited — this is primarily a display/reminder
feature ("Hole 14: Alphabet Order — A→Z"), not a hard gate.

**Variable hole count**
Already handled: `events.hole_count` is configurable at event creation (default 18).
Setting it to 16 or any other value works in v1 with no further changes needed.

**Additional formats**
Stableford, Medal/Stroke play, 2-person Ambrose — deferred. The format enum has
the slot. Build when a paying organizer asks for it.
